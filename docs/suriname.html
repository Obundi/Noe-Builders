The Story

Into the heart of the tropical forest.

Suriname is one of the most forested countries on earth — over 90% of its territory is covered by tropical rainforest, making it one of the last true wilderness areas in the world. It is here that we find some of the finest hardwoods available — timber of extraordinary density, durability, and character.

Getting to these forests requires real commitment. We travel by river, on foot, and by road to reach the source. We work directly with local partners who know the forest intimately — and who share our commitment to responsible, selective harvesting that allows the forest to regenerate.

The Wood

Timber selected log by log.

We do not buy timber from a catalogue. We go to the source, walk through the forest, and select each log ourselves. We look at the grain, test the density, check for defects, and assess the species against the requirements of each specific project.

This is time-consuming. It is expensive. And it is completely non-negotiable for us. Because the quality of the final building depends entirely on the quality of the material — and the quality of the material depends on how honestly it was chosen.

What We Do in Suriname

Timber sourcing & responsible harvesting.

01

Hardwood Sourcing

We source Class I and Class II tropical hardwoods — Ulin, Merbau, Basralocus — directly from certified forest operators in Suriname's interior.

02

Responsible Harvesting

We work only with operators who practice selective harvesting — taking individual trees rather than clearing areas, allowing the forest to regenerate naturally.

03

River Transport

Suriname's rivers are the highways of the interior. Timber is floated downstream to collection points before being processed and shipped to our workshop partners.

Suriname · Raw Material

The raw log.

This is where everything begins — a single timber log, freshly selected from the Surinamese forest. Inside this bark is a story of decades of growth, stored energy, and extraordinary material.

The density, the grain, the colour — all determined by years of growth in one of the world's most biodiverse ecosystems. We handle each log with respect for what it is, and what it is about to become.
