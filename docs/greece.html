The Story

Wool from the hillsides of Greece.

Greece is where our relationship with wool begins. On the hillside farms of the Greek countryside — where olive trees have grown for centuries and flocks have grazed the same land for generations — we find the raw material that makes our buildings breathe.

Wool is not just insulation to us. It is a living material — one that regulates humidity, absorbs sound, repels odours, and keeps the people inside warm without a single synthetic chemical. We source it here because the quality is unmatched and the connection to the land is real.

The Land

A landscape that shapes the material.

The Greek landscape is one of the most distinctive on earth — ancient olive groves, snow-capped mountains, turquoise coastlines, and dry golden hills where sheep roam freely. It is a land shaped by thousands of years of human craft and natural harmony.

When we source wool here, we are not just buying a material. We are buying into a way of life — one where animals, land, and people have learned to exist together over millennia. That story lives in every fibre.

Greece · Natural Material

The wool that insulates.

Wool has been used in construction for thousands of years — and for good reason. It is breathable, self-extinguishing, and actively improves indoor air quality by absorbing moisture and pollutants.

Unlike synthetic insulation, wool is completely non-toxic, biodegradable, and deeply connected to the land it came from. Meet the animal behind the material.
